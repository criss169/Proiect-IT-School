Pasta Recipes

Spaghetti Bolognese

Spaghetti Bolognese is a classic Italian pasta dish. It features al dente spaghetti tossed in a rich and flavorful Bolognese sauce made with ground beef, tomatoes, onions, garlic, and herbs. This hearty and satisfying dish is perfect for family dinners and gatherings.

To make Spaghetti Bolognese, cook the spaghetti according to package instructions. In a separate pan, brown the ground beef with onions and garlic. Add crushed tomatoes, tomato paste, herbs, and let the sauce simmer for about 30 minutes.

Fettuccine Alfredo

Fettuccine Alfredo is a creamy and indulgent pasta dish originating from Italy. It consists of long, flat fettuccine noodles coated in a velvety sauce made with butter, cream, and Parmesan cheese. This luxurious dish is simple yet incredibly satisfying.

To prepare Fettuccine Alfredo, cook the fettuccine noodles until al dente. In a separate saucepan, melt butter and add cream, stirring until it thickens slightly. Stir in grated Parmesan cheese, salt, and pepper. Toss the cooked fettuccine in the sauce until well coated. Serve hot with a sprinkle of fresh parsley.

Carbonara

Carbonara is a classic Roman pasta dish known for its creamy and flavorful sauce. It typically consists of spaghetti or rigatoni noodles tossed in a sauce made with eggs, cheese (usually Pecorino Romano or Parmesan), pancetta or bacon, and black pepper.

To prepare Carbonara, cook the pasta until al dente. Meanwhile, sauté pancetta or bacon until crispy. In a bowl, whisk together eggs, grated cheese, black pepper, and a little pasta water. Drain the cooked pasta and add it to the pan with the pancetta. Turn off the heat and pour in the egg mixture, tossing the pasta until coated.

Lasagna

Lasagna is a popular Italian baked pasta dish made with layers of wide pasta sheets, meat sauce, cheese, and béchamel sauce. It is a hearty and comforting dish that is perfect for feeding a crowd or for leftovers.

To make Lasagna, cook the lasagna noodles until al dente. In a separate pan, brown ground beef with onions and garlic. Add crushed tomatoes, herbs, and simmer the meat sauce. In a baking dish, layer the noodles, meat sauce, grated cheese, and béchamel sauce. Repeat the layers and bake in the oven until the cheese is melted and bubbly. Let it rest before serving.

Aglio e Olio

Aglio e Olio, meaning "garlic and oil" in Italian, is a simple and flavorful pasta dish. It consists of spaghetti tossed in olive oil, garlic, red pepper flakes, and parsley. Despite its simplicity, it packs a punch of flavor.

To prepare Aglio e Olio, cook the spaghetti until al dente. In a skillet, heat olive oil and sauté minced garlic and red pepper flakes until fragrant. Toss the cooked spaghetti in the garlic-infused oil. Finish with chopped parsley, a sprinkle of grated Parmesan cheese, and additional red pepper flakes if desired.

Pasta Primavera

Pasta Primavera is a vibrant and fresh pasta dish loaded with seasonal vegetables. It typically includes colorful vegetables like bell peppers, carrots, zucchini, broccoli, and cherry tomatoes, sautéed in olive oil and tossed with pasta.

To make Pasta Primavera, cook the pasta until al dente. In a skillet, sauté the vegetables in olive oil until tender-crisp. Season with salt, pepper, and herbs of your choice. Toss the cooked pasta with the sautéed vegetables and drizzle with a little olive oil.

Penne al Forno Siciliane

Penne al Forno Siciliane is a delicious Sicilian baked pasta dish. It features penne pasta tossed in a rich tomato sauce with eggplant, mozzarella cheese, and Parmesan cheese. The dish is then baked until the cheese is golden and bubbly.

To make Penne al Forno Siciliane, cook the penne pasta until al dente. In a separate pan, sauté diced eggplant until golden. Add crushed tomatoes, garlic, basil, salt, and pepper to make the sauce. Combine the cooked penne with the sauce and transfer it to a baking dish. Top with slices of mozzarella cheese and grated Parmesan cheese. Bake in the oven at 375°F (190°C) for about 20 minutes.

Spaghetti Milanese

Spaghetti Milanese is a classic Italian pasta dish hailing from the city of Milan. It features spaghetti noodles tossed in a rich and creamy saffron-infused sauce, topped with a breaded and fried veal cutlet known as "cotoletta".

To make Spaghetti Milanese, cook the spaghetti until al dente. Meanwhile, prepare the saffron sauce by heating cream, saffron threads, salt, and pepper in a saucepan. Let the sauce simmer until it thickens slightly. For the cotoletta, bread and fry a veal cutlet until golden and crispy. Drain the spaghetti, toss it in the saffron sauce, and serve it topped with the cotoletta.
