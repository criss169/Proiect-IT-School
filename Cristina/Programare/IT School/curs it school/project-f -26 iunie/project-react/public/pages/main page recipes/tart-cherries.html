Cherry Tart

Tart cherries have a unique flavor profile that adds a delightful tanginess to various culinary creations. Whether enjoyed in traditional desserts, incorporated into savory dishes, or consumed for potential health benefits, tart cherries offer a distinctive and versatile ingredient for cooking and baking.

Recipe Details

Prep Time: 30 minutes Cook Time: 90 minutes Total Time: 120 minutes Servings: 6
  • In a food processor, combine the all-purpose flour, granulated sugar, and salt. Add the cold cubed butter and pulse until the mixture resembles coarse crumbs.
  • Gradually add the ice water, 1 tablespoon at a time, while pulsing, until the dough comes together. Shape the dough into a disk, wrap it in plastic wrap, and refrigerate for at least 1 hour.
  • Preheat the oven to 375°F (190°C). On a lightly floured surface, roll out the chilled dough to fit a 9-inch tart pan. Press the dough into the pan and trim any excess.
  • In a large bowl, combine the pitted cherries, granulated sugar, cornstarch, lemon juice, and vanilla extract. Toss gently to coat the cherries.
  • Pour the cherry mixture into the prepared tart shell, spreading it evenly.
  • Brush the edges of the tart with beaten egg wash and sprinkle the coarse sugar over the edges.
  • Bake the tart in the preheated oven for 35-40 minutes or until the crust is golden brown and the cherries are bubbling.
  • Remove the tart from the oven and let it cool on a wire rack. Serve the cherry tart warm or at room temperature.
